## 3. Fidelity rationale

Reference doc (`322R001 Description.md`) prescribes a full 1D adiabatic PFR (tanks-in-series) with coupled species/energy ODEs, Frejacques kinetics, interphase mass-transfer flux $J_i a_i$. **Not adopted.** Project-wide pattern (HPCC, stripper, ejector) = reduced split-fraction calibrated EXACTLY to the shared design HMB. The reactor reuses that method: fixed reaction extents + pinned product vectors tuned so that at design throughput the model reproduces the two shared product HMBs bit-for-bit. This satisfies the user's binding test ("compare to shared products; if identical, bind") which the full-ODE route cannot guarantee.

The physical/thermodynamic equations from the reference doc are retained as **documentation context** (chemistry, ΔH, adiabatic balance) and govern the temperature mapping, but are not numerically integrated.

---

## 4. Chemistry

Two reactions, extents $\xi_U,\xi_B$ (kmol/h):

$$2\,\mathrm{NH_3} + \mathrm{CO_2} \;\rightarrow\; \mathrm{NH_2CONH_2} + \mathrm{H_2O} \qquad (\xi_U)$$

$$2\,\mathrm{NH_2CONH_2} \;\rightarrow\; \mathrm{NH_2CONHCONH_2} + \mathrm{NH_3} \qquad (\xi_B)$$

Heats (context only, not integrated — adiabatic vessel): $\Delta H_{carb}\approx-160\;\mathrm{kJ/mol}$ (carbamate formation, exothermic), $\Delta H_{urea}\approx+15.5\;\mathrm{kJ/mol}$ (urea dehydration, endothermic). Net mole change: urea rxn $\Delta n=-1$ per event ($3\to2$); biuret rxn $\Delta n=0$.

Stoichiometric vectors $\nu_{i,U}$, $\nu_{i,B}$:

$$\nu_{i,U}=\{\mathrm{NH_3}\!:\!-2,\ \mathrm{CO_2}\!:\!-1,\ \mathrm{Urea}\!:\!+1,\ \mathrm{H_2O}\!:\!+1\},\qquad
\nu_{i,B}=\{\mathrm{Urea}\!:\!-2,\ \mathrm{Biuret}\!:\!+1,\ \mathrm{NH_3}\!:\!+1\}$$

Design extents (calibrated to shared HMB via mass-closure probe):

$$\xi_{U,\mathrm{des}}=1302.27\;\mathrm{kmol/h},\qquad \xi_{B,\mathrm{des}}=2.414\;\mathrm{kmol/h}$$

## 5. Throughput scaling

Reuse project throughput ratio (same `co2_scale` as stripper/HPCC):

$$s=\frac{\dot m_{CO_2,\mathrm{feed}}}{\dot m_{CO_2,\mathrm{des}}}=\frac{\texttt{co2\_feed\_th}}{\texttt{CO2\_DES\_KGH}/1000}$$

Extents scale linearly: $\xi_U=\xi_{U,\mathrm{des}}\,s$, $\xi_B=\xi_{B,\mathrm{des}}\,s$.

## 6. Product equations (Approach A — pinned)

Products pinned to shared design HMB, scaled by throughput $s$ and (overflow only) valve modulation $\phi/\phi_{des}$:

$$\boxed{\;\dot n^{ov}_i=\nu^{ov}_{i,\mathrm{des}}\cdot s\cdot\frac{\phi}{\phi_{des}}\;}\qquad
\boxed{\;\dot n^{og}_i=\nu^{og}_{i,\mathrm{des}}\cdot s\;}$$

$\phi$ = HIC-322605 opening fraction; $\phi_{des}$ = design opening (§7). At design ($s=1,\ \phi=\phi_{des}$) the products equal the shared HMBs exactly.

**Design overflow vector** $\nu^{ov}_{\mathrm{des}}$ (kmol/h) — TT-322014, → 322E001; Σ = 8427.11, mass 226 178 kg/h:

| Urea | Biuret | NH3 | CO2 | H2O | N2 | O2 | CH4 | H2 |
|------|--------|-----|-----|-----|----|----|----|----|
| 1302.6 | 2.414 | 4002.4 | 897.7 | 2222.0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 |

**Design off-gas vector** $\nu^{og}_{\mathrm{des}}$ (kmol/h) — TT-322009, → 322E003; Σ = 963.76 (published HMB total 963.85; mol% rounded to 2 dp ⇒ Σ mol% = 99.99), mass 22 355 kg/h, $\overline{MW}$ 23.19, ρ 113.30 kg/m³:

## 7. HV-322605 valve model

Datasheet (Uhde 01-3042/HELWAN, tag HV 322605): angle control valve DN200 PN200, **linear inherent characteristic**, parabolic plug single seat, stroke 65 mm, Kvs ≈ 600. Design service: flow 228.0–232.5 m³/h, ρ 990 kg/m³, T_in 183 °C, P 141.9 → 141.5 bar a (ΔP 0.40 bar), phase = urea solution (L).

Cross-check anchor: overflow mass 226 178 kg/h ÷ 990 kg/m³ = **228.5 m³/h** ✓ (within datasheet flow band) ⇒ valve passes the overflow liquid; linear trim ⇒ volumetric flow ∝ opening fraction.

Design opening from liquid valve sizing (linear trim ⇒ $\phi=K_v^{req}/K_{vs}$):

$$K_v^{req}=Q_{des}\sqrt{\frac{\rho/\rho_{ref}}{\Delta P}}=228.5\sqrt{\frac{0.990}{0.40}}=359.5\;\mathrm{m^3/h}
\quad\Rightarrow\quad \phi_{des}=\frac{359.5}{600}=0.599\approx 0.60$$

**HIC-322605 default setpoint = 60 % open.** Overflow draw ∝ $\phi$ (linear). At $\phi=\phi_{des}=0.60$, overflow = design (modulation factor 1). $\phi$ is an operator-manipulable input; off-design $\phi$ rescales the overflow draw (steady what-if — level integration not modelled, see §1/§10).

**Binding:** equation §6 overflow is bound to HV-322605 (factor $\phi/\phi_{des}$). Feed equation bound to 322E002 (consumes `hpcc["feed_kmolh"]`).

## 8. Closure diagnostic (reported, NOT injected)

Live feed (`hpcc["feed_kmolh"]`, 10 943.7 kmol/h at design) is ~2.3 % richer than products + reaction demand (10 693.1 kmol/h). Residual is a structural artifact of piecewise per-unit calibration (recycle loop not globally converged), not a real plant imbalance. Approach A keeps both products pinned to ground-truth HMB and surfaces the gap as an auditable diagnostic:

$$R=\sum_i \dot n^{feed}_i-\Big(\sum_i \dot n^{ov}_i+\sum_i \dot n^{og}_i\Big)-\xi_U \;\approx\; +250\,s\;\mathrm{kmol/h}\;\;(2.3\%)$$

($-\xi_U$ accounts for the net mole loss of the urea reaction.) Exposed in packet as `closure_resid`. Bounded < 3 % of feed = acceptance guard. **Not added to any product** (would corrupt the matched HMBs).
