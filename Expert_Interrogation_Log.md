**Q — Which documented real-world Stamicarbon disturbances did you research, and what do the sources say should happen?**

Five documented upset classes were researched with URLs. (1) LOSS OF PASSIVATION AIR / O2: BC Insight 'Problem No. 18: HP stripper passivation air' documents that austenitic 25-22-2 tolerates 30-40 min but 'most licensors recommend a maximum time of 10-15 minutes only'; specifically for Stamicarbon plants the rule is that the plant 'must shut down if oxygen percentage is less than 0.5 vol-% in the CO2 feed for 15 minutes'. ## 6. Closing ruling

The panel is right that TD-004 and TD-005 are the highest-risk code in the repo, and both self-certifications fail on execution: **TD-005 creates mass (I reproduced it two ways), and TD-004 certified a windup ramp as a cascade response using a setpoint step larger than the loop's entire authority.** But the most dangerous finding is neither — it is that **the code was unstable at the step size the training mode actually uses, and both gates were structurally incapable of noticing.** Fix the integrator, stop the mass leak, then close the gate. Everything else is fidelity work that can wait for an engine you can trust to measure itself.

One thing worth protecting: H's independent 79.97 % vs 80 % stripper-efficiency validation is genuine, out-of-sample, and currently defended by nothing. It says the steady-state physics is sound and the rot is confined to the dynamic and off-design paths. That is a much better position to be in than this report's blocker count suggests.
